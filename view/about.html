A little bit about me

My name is Miryam, I come from Tunisia, and I work as a Junior developer for a startup in Munich, Germany. I have been writing code passionately since 2006, and professionally since 2016.

Since I was a kid, I was always curious to understand how things worked. I thought studying physics would give me the universal answer, but unfortunately it didn't. On the flipside, I discovered a slightly simpler universe to grasp, that of computers.

At the end of my studies, I became fully committed to becoming a software developer, and all the learning and studying turned from a chore to a pleasure. When I graduated, I thought naively that the education phase was done. But reality quickly caught up with me, and I realized that being a developer meant never-ending learning.

The joy of ignorance

Fortunately, we live in a time where information is only a few key strokes away. I am also surrounded by an amazing team of experienced developers, which are kind enough to share their knowledge with me. I even sometimes give myself a little challenge, for instance by taking certification exams, or going to workshops on topics which I find a bit scary.

But this infinite amount of information can sometimes be confusing, and a little discouraging. By attending numerous conferences and Meetups on software, I gathered a decent amount of knowledge on topics which fascinated me, and I isolated those which would be more pertinent for my day to day work.

Attending the Software Architecture Conference would be a very valuable experience to me, and I hopefully I could share the knwolege I would gather there with my colleagues.
